Level 32 · Anchor Piece: Hostage/Handcuffs

Three Fugitives

Wrest control of the hostage transfer, file down the cuffs one link at a time, and usher the kid to the safe balcony before the patrol grid tightens.

Stage Notes

An exasperated ex-con is tethered to a frantic bank robber and her wide-eyed daughter. The kid is the hostage piece; she refuses to move unless nudged by the Fugitive. Police units sweep in from the perimeter the moment the bank alarms chirp.

  • Handcuff links: Two alloy braces pin the Fugitive to a tiny alley. Forge a Filing Stone to cut each link before you can reposition for the push.
  • Elemental stones: Sparkstone and Gritrock are stashed nearby. Carry both into the Filing Tray to shape a fresh cutter when needed.
  • Police flow: Until the cuffs are gone, patrols dog the Fugitive. Once both links are severed they refocus on the hostage, so plan to trigger the Distraction during your escape push—it still traps them in loops for three of your turns.
  • Fragile hostage: The kid can’t cross walls or cops. Plan each shove so she never meets a moving patrol.

Opening Maneuver

The scenario eases up once you keep the cops orbiting you instead of the hostage. Use this loop to secure the resources and slice the cuffs before they can tighten the net.

  1. Swing south first to grab the Gritrock cache, then hug the left wall to climb to the Sparkstone stash. The patrols will tail the Fugitive, so keep moving and don’t let them pin you against the walls.
  2. Lead them south, then cut north to the Filing Tray to forge and sever each link. Reforge immediately so both cuffs are open before you reposition.
  3. Fire the Distraction once the second link is cut, shove the hostage east into the open lane, then sprint around to push her north three times into the safe balcony.
