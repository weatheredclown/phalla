Wrestle the Griswold blueprint into a dazzling reality. Route every string, dodge crossed wires, and keep the panel fed before the timer fuses your holiday spectacular.

Stage Notes

The Griswold house is ready for its annual overkill. A master timer ticks down while you snake wires from the main breaker to every socket. Each phase unlocks new wings of the house—finish the porch, swing through the garage, stabilize the first floor, then conquer the neon roofline.

  • Staples: Every segment you punch into the siding burns through your staple stash. Run it dry and the crew has to reload, wasting precious seconds.
  • Wire Lengths: Each bundle has a sweet spot. Overshoot the ideal path and you’ll pay extra staples for the slack you have to hide.
  • Overloaded Junctions: Risk the volatile attic taps for massive multipliers, but be ready to sprint back when they short out the whole section.
  • Blueprint Discipline: Wires cannot cross. If another string is already stapled to a route, you’ll have to find a cleaner line.

Phase Rundown

  1. Front Door Warm-Up: Ease in with the porch columns and archway.
  2. Garage Glow-Up: The side bays open with tighter runs and minimal slack.
  3. First-Floor Frenzy: Living room windows and den corners demand perfect routing.
  4. Roof Reckoning: Overloaded attic taps fuel the roofline finale—just pray they hold.

Risk & Reward

The attic junctions pay 2× wattage when they behave, but each completion has a chance to blow with a deafening ZAP!. Shorted sections drop offline until you route them again, wasting staples and time.

Controls

Start / Pause
Enter or the on-screen buttons to control the timer.
Route Segment
Click blueprint nodes to extend the active wire. You can also use the arrow keys to move from node to node.
Undo
Backspace or the Undo button to pull the last staple.
Cancel Wire
Esc aborts the current run and returns the spool to the bin.
